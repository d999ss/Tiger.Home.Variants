## About the Role

Tiger BioSciences is seeking an exceptional Senior Scientist to lead groundbreaking research initiatives in advanced tissue engineering and biomaterial development. This role sits at the intersection of basic science and clinical translation, driving innovation in regenerative medicine technologies that improve patient outcomes worldwide.

You will direct research programs focused on developing next-generation tissue-based products, working collaboratively with cross-functional teams including R&D, clinical affairs, regulatory, and manufacturing. This position offers the opportunity to shape the scientific direction of our tissue engineering platform and contribute to products that make a meaningful difference in patients' lives.

## Key Responsibilities

- Lead tissue engineering research programs from concept through preclinical validation
- Design and execute experiments to characterize biomaterial properties and biological interactions
- Develop novel processing methodologies to preserve native tissue architecture and bioactivity
- Collaborate with clinical teams to translate research findings into evidence-based product improvements
- Author technical reports, regulatory submissions, and peer-reviewed publications
- Mentor junior scientists and research associates in experimental design and execution
- Present research findings to internal stakeholders and at scientific conferences
- Maintain current knowledge of scientific literature and competitive landscape

## Required Qualifications

- PhD in Bioengineering, Biomedical Engineering, Cell Biology, or related discipline
- Minimum 5 years of industry experience in tissue engineering or biomaterials research
- Deep expertise in extracellular matrix biology and tissue scaffolding technologies
- Proven track record of scientific publications in peer-reviewed journals
- Strong understanding of regulatory requirements for tissue-based products (FDA 361 HCT/P)
- Experience with aseptic processing and cGMP manufacturing principles
- Proficiency with tissue characterization techniques (histology, immunohistochemistry, mechanical testing)
- Excellent written and verbal communication skills

## Preferred Qualifications

- Experience with allograft tissue processing and preservation
- Knowledge of wound healing biology and regenerative medicine applications
- Track record of successful product development from research through commercialization
- Experience managing research budgets and external collaborations
- Familiarity with biostatistics and experimental design for preclinical studies
